# Strawberry Riff — Platform Experience Bible
### The Eighth Bible · How the Platform Feels to Move Through

> "The goal isn't conscious recognition. It's felt experience. People won't leave
> Strawberry Riff thinking 'the retention banner language was philosophically
> misaligned.' They'll just feel, somewhere they can't name, that something was off.
> And they won't come back. The inverse is equally true — they'll just feel, somewhere
> underneath the thinking, that this place gets them. And they will come back.
> And they'll bring someone."

---

## Prime Axiom (inherited from Universe Visual Constitution)

Transcendence radiates outward into connection, intimacy, and self-reflection — all
as nested scales of the same field.

**Applied to UX:** Every interaction — a button click, a loading state, a placeholder
word, a transition — is either building toward that field or breaking it. There is no
neutral. Every element is either earning trust or spending it.

---

## The Eight-Question Diagnostic

Run every screen against these eight questions before launch and after every
significant change:

1. **Breath** — Does this screen have an inhale and an exhale? Is there rhythm, or
   is it a wall of information?
2. **Safety** — Does the user feel held, not pressured? Is the first message
   invitation or demand?
3. **Sincerity** — Does the language sound like a person who means it, or a
   platform that wants something?
4. **Continuity** — Does this screen share DNA with every other screen? Would a
   user recognize it as Strawberry Riff if the logo were removed?
5. **Language** — Is every word earned? Is there a single phrase that sounds like
   it came from a SaaS template?
6. **Residue** — What feeling does the user carry away? Does it match the prime
   axiom?
7. **Transcendence** — Is there at least one moment on this screen that reaches
   beyond utility into something felt?
8. **Inhabited** — Does this feel like a world people live in, or a template waiting
   to be filled?

## Chapter 1 — The Arrival Contract (Landing Page)

The landing page is not the first impression. If the songs transmit the universe before
the page is seen, the page just needs to honor what the song already started.

**Laws:**
- The atmosphere must be present from the first pixel. World before features.
- Language above the fold must land in the body, not the brain. "Heartbeat-driven"
  earns its place. "Everything You Need" does not.
- CTA hierarchy must be ordered by creative invitation, not conversion pressure.
  "Join the Tribe" is belonging language, not signup language.
- Human voices (Sonic Soulprints) must appear near the hero — before the feature
  grid. Humans make a space inhabited. Features make it a product.
- The parasympathetic baseline needs runway before the platform sells itself. One
  section of emotional resonance before features land.
- Dead empty sections are forbidden. The Constitution: "dead sterile emptiness"
  breaks the breath of the page.

**Amber items (current):**
- "Everything You Need to Share Your Sound" → replace with warmer copy
- Sonic Soulprints buried below features → move one near the hero
- Feature icons feel generic → carry more of the platform's warmth

## Chapter 2 — The Onboarding Initiation

Open with a question, not a form.

**The question:** "What does music do for you?" with three options that feel like
personality, not data collection:
- *It carries what I can't say out loud*
- *It's how I think*
- *It's how I connect with people*

This question does something before a single note is generated: it tells the user that
this platform sees them as a person with a relationship to music, not a user with a
genre preference.

**Laws:**
- The first thing a new user sees should be a question, not a form.
- Onboarding should feel like an initiation, not a registration.
- Every field should feel like a conversation. Not a command line.

## Chapter 3 — The Sacred Space of Creation (Generate / Studio)

The Generate page is a creative venue. It must feel like one.

**Laws:**
- The prompt field should feel like a conversation, not a command line.
  - Current: `"e.g., Midnight Blues"` → functional
  - Target: `"What is this song carrying?"` or `"What does it already know?"` — even
    alongside the example. Small change, large emotional shift.
- The venue image should respond subtly to the selected venue/mood. Even a gentle
  color temperature shift makes the space feel alive and responsive rather than static.
- Loading is an inhale, not dead time. When generation is running, the user should
  see something becoming. Even a single warm line — *"something is becoming"* —
  transforms the experience.
- "Stems" as a button label undersells the feature. "Reveal Stems" or an icon that
  suggests opening/revealing honors Stem Splitter Law 1.

### Stem Splitter Laws

**Law 1 — Reveal, don't extract.**
The stems are not being pulled out of a file. They are being uncovered from inside
the music. Language and UI must honor this. "What's inside this track" not
"Individual Stems." "Reveal" not "Download."

**Law 2 — The beat of stillness.**
The moment of separation — when the stems first appear — needs a beat of visual
stillness before controls appear. The user should have one breath to register what
just happened before they are asked to do anything. This is the highest-value design
opportunity on the platform. It transforms a technical feature into a philosophical one.

**Law 3 — The stems are creative DNA.**
Language around retention, expiry, or deletion must never frame the stems as
temporary files. They are the user's creative material. Any retention messaging must
be attached to the solution (save to keep forever), never to the threat (deleted in 30
days).

## Chapter 4 — The Philosophy Page (About)

The About page is where the universe declares itself. It is currently the strongest
screen on the platform.

**What to protect:**
- "Music begins with a heartbeat. From a kid drumming on a desk..." — never change
  this opening. It is the Writer's Bible's sensory specificity at its best.
- "Support over status. Feedback over follow-counts. Tribe over traffic." — triple
  contrast structure. These are arguments, not values statements.
- "You can ring our bell" / "We read every note" — warm, specific, slightly poetic.
  The sincerity standard applied to customer service.
- "Artificial intelligence here is a sidekick, never the star." — the right framing. It
  differentiates Strawberry Riff from every other generator on the market.

**What needs tuning:**
- "We're building the future of music creation" → generic subtitle. Every other line
  is specific. This one sounds like a press release. Rewrite in Riff voice.
- Jam Session / band member section → visually disconnected. If these are mascots,
  they need more presence and personality. If they're placeholders, they need
  resolution before launch.

## Chapter 5 — The Discovery Experience (Discover)

The Discover page is where the community becomes visible to itself.

**What to protect:**
- "Fresh drops from the community — curated by humans, not math." — never change
  this subtitle. It is the platform's entire philosophy in twelve words.
- Vibe filter language (emotional states, not genre tags) — the user is searching by
  feeling, not category. This is the philosophy made into navigation.
- Shuffle button — "Surprise Me" as a primary navigation option. It says: we trust
  the community's music enough to hand you something random.

**What needs work:**
- Gradient placeholder cards feel staged, not inhabited. Generated cover art (even
  abstract, from the Cover Art Bible framework) would transform this page.
- Like counts without emotional framing. "165 people felt this" lands differently
  than "♡ 165."

**Language principles:**
- "Your vibe:" not "Vibe:" — the listener owns the filter
- "Play My Mix" when tags are active, "Play All Riffs" when none are selected
- "Reset my mix" not "Clear"
- Tag click = "Filter by this vibe" (tooltip)
- Empty state: "No riffs match this vibe yet — try a different mix"

## Chapter 6 — The Creator's Home (My Riffs)

My Riffs is where the creator sees themselves reflected.

**Laws:**
- The creator's catalog is their identity. Every element should reinforce ownership,
  not management.
- "Your Creative Identity" — the vibe fingerprint — should be accessible from the
  top of the page, not just the bottom.
- Edit actions should be unambiguous. No double-click confusion. No navigation
  accidents.

---

## Chapter 7 — The Sharing Moment

What makes a Strawberry Riff track shareable vs. merely good?

**The principle:** When someone shares a track, they are not sharing a file. They are
sharing a feeling they had and want someone else to have. The sharing mechanism
must honor that.

**Laws:**
- The share action should feel like an invitation, not a copy-paste.
- The shared link should land on the creator's track, not a platform feed.
  The creator gets first right of refusal on the listener's attention.
- "More from [Creator]" before "More like this" — always.
- Auto-play after a shared link is forbidden. The listener chose to come here.
  Let them stay here.

## Chapter 8 — The Language Standard

Every word on the platform is either earning trust or spending it.

**Forbidden phrases:**
- "Everything You Need" — SaaS template language
- "We're building the future of..." — press release language
- "Download your files or lose them" — threat language
- Any countdown to deletion in primary UI
- "Stems" as a flat action label (undersells the feature)

**Earned phrases (protect these):**
- "Curated by humans, not math"
- "Heartbeat-driven sound"
- "AI is a sidekick, never the star"
- "Your content, your control"
- "That is where the Riff lives"
- "You can ring our bell"
- "Join the Tribe" (not "Sign Up")
- "Play My Mix" (not "Play All")
- "Your vibe:" (not "Vibe:")
- "Saved mixes are yours forever"

**The voice test:** Would the About page's opening paragraph recognize this line as
belonging to the same universe? If not, rewrite it.

## The Breath Metric — Applied to Every Screen

Every screen should have this arc, even if compressed:

| Phase | What it is | Platform example |
|---|---|---|
| **Inhale** | Parasympathetic baseline | Venue image, warm background, soft arrival |
| **Swell** | Mythic elevation | The track title, the waveform, the creative invitation |
| **Crest** | Emotional peak | The generation moment, the stem reveal, the first play |
| **Release** | Frisson or catharsis | The track plays, the stems separate, the lyrics appear |
| **Stillness** | The locked final frame | Loading complete, save confirmation, share confirmation |
| **Residue** | Belonging | The feeling carried away from the interaction |
